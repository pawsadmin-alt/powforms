Pet Owner's Bill of Rights

As a client of our mobile veterinary clinic, you and your pet have the following rights:

  • The Right to Compassionate and Respectful Care: You have the right to expect that your pet will be treated with compassion, dignity, and respect at all times. Our staff will treat your pet as if it were our own.
  • The Right to Professional and Competent Service: You have the right to expect that our veterinarians and staff are licensed, qualified, and will provide the highest standard of professional care as they are able to. If we are unable to, you have the right to be referred to a higher level of care.
  • The Right to Information and Informed Consent: You have the right to be fully informed about your pet's health status, including a diagnosis (if possible), the purpose of recommended procedures or treatments, and any associated risks or costs. You have the right to ask questions and have them answered in a way you can understand. We will not perform any non-emergency procedures or treatments without your informed consent.
  • The Right to a Clear Treatment Plan and Cost Estimate: Before any significant procedure or treatment, you have the right to a clear explanation of the proposed treatment plan and a reasonable estimate of the costs involved. We will inform you of any changes to the plan or costs as they arise.
  • The Right to Confidentiality and Privacy: All information regarding you and your pet's medical history will be kept confidential and will not be released to any third party without your explicit consent, except as required by law.
  • The Right to Access Medical Records: You have the right to request and receive copies of your pet's medical records in a timely manner. We will assist you in transferring these records to another veterinarian upon your request.
  • The Right to a Safe and Hygienic Environment: You have the right to expect that our mobile clinic will arrive in a clean, sanitized, and professional condition. We are committed to maintaining a sterile and safe environment to minimize the risk of infection.
  • The Right to Voice Concerns: You have the right to voice your concerns or complaints regarding our services. We take all feedback seriously and are committed to resolving issues promptly and fairly. However, all communication must be respectful and in accordance with our Zero Tolerance Policy for Abuse of Staff.

By choosing our mobile veterinary clinic, you entrust us with the care of your beloved pet. We honor that trust and are dedicated to upholding these rights in every interaction.

Force Majeure Clause

Section 1: Force Majeure Events

The performance of our services may be suspended or delayed without liability to you in the event of any unforeseen circumstances beyond our reasonable control (a “Force Majeure Event”). Such events include, but are not limited to:

  • Natural Disasters: Fires, floods, earthquakes, hurricanes, or other acts of God.
  • Weather and Road Conditions: Severe weather (e.g., blizzards, ice storms, heavy rain) that makes travel unsafe or impossible, as well as significant road closures, accidents, or other public safety issues.
  • Public Health Emergencies: Epidemics, pandemics, quarantines, or other public health crises that restrict movement or create health risks for our staff.
  • Governmental Actions: Acts of government, including changes in law, curfews, or mandatory closures.
  • Civil Unrest: Riots, strikes, lockouts, or other civil disturbances.
  • Vehicle Malfunctions: Unexpected and severe mechanical failure of our mobile veterinary unit.

Section 2: Notification and Mitigation

In the event of a Force Majeure Event, we will make reasonable efforts to notify you as soon as possible and reschedule your appointment without penalty. We will also take reasonable steps to mitigate the impact of the event and resume normal operations as soon as it is safe and practical to do so.

Section 3: Termination

If a Force Majeure Event persists for an extended period, rendering the delivery of services impractical or impossible, we reserve the right to terminate our agreement with you without liability. In such a case, any prepaid fees for services not yet rendered will be refunded.

Zero Tolerance Policy: Respectful and Safe Environment

We are committed to providing a safe, respectful, and professional environment for our staff and clients. We have a zero-tolerance policy for any form of verbal, emotional, or physical abuse directed towards our employees. This includes, but is not limited to:

  • Verbal Abuse: Yelling, threats, offensive language, or any form of harassment.
  • Intimidation: Aggressive gestures, threatening behavior, or any actions intended to intimidate or frighten our staff.
  • Physical Abuse: Any form of physical violence, assault, or unwanted physical contact.
  • Cyberbullying and Defamation: Sending derogatory emails, making harassing phone calls, or posting offensive, false, or malicious comments about our staff on social media, review sites, or any other online platform. This includes any actions that may harm the reputation of our employees or our business.

We believe that our staff deserves to work in a safe environment, free from fear and intimidation. Our employees are dedicated professionals who are here to provide the best possible care for your pet. They are not to be subjected to abusive behavior under any circumstances.
